Piston Liner Numerical Generation

利用信号处理及统计算法数值生成发动机气缸表面

Developed statistical and fast Fourier transform algorithm to calculate key topological features from measured piston liner surfaces.

Developed algorithms based on signal processing and random theory to numerically generate liner surfaces, which can be used for piston design and optimization.

The generated liner surfaces have similar physical properties compared with real ones.

A windows based graphical user interface was developed including all the algorithms mentioned above. With the GUI, sponsors do not need to look into the complex codes, but simply click the buttons.

Technique used in this project: MATLAB, C++, Signal Processing, Statistics

Companies using my results: Shell, Volvo, Toyota, MTU, and etc.

For more details: Please refer to my graduate thesis or contact me directly.

  • Date: July 2013 - Sept. 2015
  • Adviser: Dr. Tian Tian
  • Category: Graduate Research

Statistic Models

建立的一些统计模型

Build quantitative connection between ISO 13565-2 and ISO 13565-3. Based on the combination of two gaussian distribution, modify a common understanding of the connection between a piston liner topological parameter and ISO standard.

Developed a quantitative model to simulate the piston liner wear processes based on Gaussian distribution and random theory. The simulated processes matches well with the real processes.

Visual Recognition through Machine Learning Competition

一门有意思的课程

Three of us as a team developed machine learning algorithms to predict the probability that the image contains each class of object, and compete among over 20 other teams.

The team with highest average precision would win the competition.

Our team developed algorithms based on unsupervised feature learning and SVM classifier with regularization with final average precision over 81%.

We got the first prize with fantastic awards! please refer to official website for more details.

Career Websites

一段创业经历

We developed websites for other organizations in both US and China. The following is a career fair website I developed for MIT Asian Career Fair two years ago. Since they do not use it any more, I will open source it in the near future so that career fairs may be able to develop their own websites based on it.

Employers and employees can edit their profiles, upload pdf files, and images.

Authenticated employers and employees can search each other's information on the platform.

Website admins can view the website situation and authenticate employers on the admin page.

Besides the functions mentioned above, we also provide optional functions including database backup to Google Docs, resume parsing when uploading, resume downloading in batch.

Technique used in this project: SailsJS, NodeJS, MongoDB, Jquery, AngularJS
